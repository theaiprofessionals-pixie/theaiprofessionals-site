Why this matters

AI adoption is outrunning the habits needed to use it well.

AI tools are arriving in classrooms, workplaces, and everyday decisions faster than the habits and institutions needed to use them well. Two gaps in particular motivate our work:

  • Cognitive enfeeblement. When AI can produce confident answers on demand, the small acts of reading, verifying, and thinking things through become easy to skip — and the ability to do them atrophies silently.
  • Missing investigative practice. As AI agents take more autonomous action, incidents involving misaligned behaviour will require the same kind of dedicated investigations methodology that fields like cybersecurity and insider threat have developed over decades. That methodology does not yet exist.

Both gaps share a common feature: they reward serious, grounded, practitioner-informed work over abstract commentary. That is the work we are set up to do.
